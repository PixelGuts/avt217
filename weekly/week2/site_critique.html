Navigation

The navigation bar at the top of the page is clean, simple, and easy to find. It remains static as you scroll, which is a good design decision for consistent access to navigation options, regardless of where you are on the page. The primary categories like "Things to Do," "Plan Your Trip," and "Events" are clearly labeled and intuitively cover all areas of interest for tourists or potential visitors to Washington, D.C.

Usability

The site is generally easy to navigate, with straightforward paths for both general visitors and those seeking specific information like events, places to eat, or hotels. The site loads relatively quickly, and links to subpages are easy to follow.

Typography and hierarchy

The website uses clean, modern typography that is easy to read. The font choices are legible across different screen sizes, and the font weights (bold for headlines, regular for body text) establish a clear distinction between primary and secondary information. Some areas of the site, especially on deeper pages, use very dense text (such as long paragraphs or listings). Breaking them up into smaller chunks or using more subheadings could make the content easier to scan and digest.
